## Target Roasters

### Research Criteria
- Small to medium roasters (not Blue Bottle/Stumptown scale)
- Active on social media (indicates tech-savviness)
- Currently using basic/dated e-commerce OR no online store
- Engaged community (comments, shares)
- Located in US (easier for initial support)

### How to Find Them
1. **Instagram:** Search #specialtycoffee #coffeeroaster #thirdwavecoffee
2. **Reddit:** r/roasting, r/coffee - look for roaster AMAs
3. **Google Maps:** "[City] coffee roaster" - check their websites
4. **Sprudge:** Read articles for roaster mentions
5. **Coffee Review:** Find highly-rated small roasters

## Research Tips

### Red Flags (Skip These)
- Already on Shopify Plus (too invested to switch)
- Multi-location chains (too complex for MVP)
- No social media presence (hard to reach, may not value tech)
- Recently redesigned website (not looking to change)

### Green Flags (Prioritize These)
- Complaints about current platform in posts
- Using basic Squarespace/Wix with limited features
- No subscription option but customers asking for it
- Manually managing orders via DMs/email
- Small team, wearing many hats

### Conversation Starters
- Comment genuinely on their posts before DMing
- Mention specific coffees you've tried (if true)
- Reference a recent post or achievement
- Keep it short - respect their time

## Follow-Up Sequence

**Day 0:** Initial outreach
**Day 3:** If no response, like/comment on recent post (stay visible)
**Day 7:** Brief follow-up: "Hey! Just bumping this in case it got buried. No worries if you're not interested - keep roasting great coffee!"
**Day 14:** Final follow-up or move on

---

## Notes

- Don't spam. Quality over quantity.
- Personalize every message. Generic = ignored.
- Actually try their coffee if possible (authentic connection).
- Be prepared to deliver on the free setup promise.
- Document feedback received for product improvements.
